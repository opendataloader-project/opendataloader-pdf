Plastics are categorized by food grade. 9 The six food grades are 1) Polyethylene Terephthalate: clear, tough plastic such as soft drinks, juice and water, (2) High Density Polyethylene: white or colored plastic such as milk containers, (3) Polyvinyl Chloride: hard rigid clear plastic such as cordial bottles; (4) Low Density Polyethylene: soft, flexible such as squeezable bottles; 5) Polypropylene: hard but flexible plastics such as microwave ware; takeaway containers, some yogurt or jam containers and hinged lunch boxes, and (6) Polystyrene: rigid, brittle plastics such as small tubes and margarine or butter container.
